Pressure sources in hospitals

Hospitals are frequently engaged in a race against time to save patients, which inevitably creates a high-pressure environment. This is a conclusion that our group reached after conducting a brainstorming session to analyze sources of pressure in hospitals. While our list of sources may not be exhaustive, it covers most of the significant factors that contribute to the pressure experienced in hospital settings. Among these sources of pressure, we identified waiting anxiety as a significant concern, and explored potential solutions involving social robots.
Pressure sources in hospitals

To further illustrate this point, consider a scenario where waiting rooms are overcrowded, and the waiting environment is unpleasant. The accumulation of individual and group anxiety in such situations poses a potential threat to hospital systems and may lead to negative experiences, including interpersonal conflicts. Our group believes that by examining the potential for social robots to alleviate waiting anxiety, hospitals may be able to address this pressure source and enhance the overall patient experience.
