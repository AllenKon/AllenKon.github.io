    ABSTRACT

    Social robots robots are increasingly used in hospitals to provide informational guidance and customer service. However, lack of work is done on their roles to help people alleviate anxiety people might experience while waiting for medical procedures (such as registration, result waiting and diagnostic meetings with doctors). Various environmental (e.g. interior design, facilities) and social factors (e.g. social interaction with the others) could affect the waiting experiences of patients. The accumulation of individual and group anxiety posits potential threat to the hospital systems, which might lead to bad experience or even interpersonal conflicts. This project aims to research the opportunities of designing social robots that help people avoid or alleviate people’s anxiety while waiting in the hospitals and develop the brand new type of social interaction with human and robots.

    KEYWORDS

    Human Robot Interaction, Hospital, Social Robots, Emotional interaction, Emotion-based Robot Gestures

    RESEARCH PURPOSE/QUESTIONS

    • To design a social robot (between a companion and service robot) that helps mitigate people’s waiting anxiety
    • To explore what are the ‘effective’ interactive behaviors of social robots in emotional communication and social interaction with human in waiting environments
